# RAID Levels & Drive Failure Recovery

Overview of Redundant Array of Independent Disks and how different levels balance performance, capacity, and fault tolerance.

- **RAID 0 (Striping)** — data split across disks; high performance, no redundancy (any disk failure = total data loss)
- **RAID 1 (Mirroring)** — identical data on two+ disks; excellent fault tolerance, half capacity
- **RAID 5 (Striping with Parity)** — distributed parity across 3+ disks; single-disk fault tolerance
- **RAID 6 (Striping with Double Parity)** — two parity blocks across 4+ disks; survives two simultaneous disk failures
- **RAID 10 (1+0)** — mirrored sets striped together; combines performance of RAID 0 with redundancy of RAID 1
- **Drive failure recovery**: hot-swap failed drive, auto-rebuild from parity/mirror, monitoring SMART metrics
- **Trade-offs**: cost vs. capacity vs. performance vs. protection level
