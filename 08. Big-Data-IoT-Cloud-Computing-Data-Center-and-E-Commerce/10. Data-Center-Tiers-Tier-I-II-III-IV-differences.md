# Data Center Tiers — Tier I, II, III & IV Differences

Standard classification system (Uptime Institute) defining data center reliability and redundancy.

- **Tier I (Basic Capacity)**
  - Single path for power and cooling; no redundancy
  - Annual downtime: ~28.8 hours (99.671% availability)
- **Tier II (Redundant Capacity Components)**
  - Single path with redundant components (N+1 for critical equipment)
  - Annual downtime: ~22 hours (99.741% availability)
- **Tier III (Concurrently Maintainable)**
  - Multiple power/cooling paths; any component can be removed without shutdown
  - N+1 redundancy; annual downtime: ~1.6 hours (99.982% availability)
- **Tier IV (Fault Tolerant)**
  - Multiple active paths; tolerance to any single equipment failure
  - 2N redundancy (fully duplicated); annual downtime: ~26 minutes (99.995% availability)
- **Key factors**: redundancy level, maintenance capability, fault tolerance, cost
