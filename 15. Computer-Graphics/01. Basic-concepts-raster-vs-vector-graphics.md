# Basic Concepts — Raster vs Vector Graphics

Fundamental concepts of computer graphics covering the two main image representation models. Includes display pipelines, color models, and resolution concepts.

- **Raster Graphics** — pixel-based representation (bitmaps), resolution-dependent, suitable for photorealistic images
- **Vector Graphics** — mathematical curves and shapes (paths), resolution-independent, suitable for logos and illustrations
- **Coordinate Systems** — screen coordinates, world coordinates, and normalized device coordinates
- **Color Models** — RGB, CMYK, HSV, and color depth (bits per pixel)
- **Rendering Pipeline** — from geometry to pixels, scan conversion
